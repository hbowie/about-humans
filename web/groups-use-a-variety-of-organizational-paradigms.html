38. Groups use a variety of organizational paradigms.

We’ve already touched on the importance of cooperative groups in human life, and we’ve spoken of culture as providing the operating systems for groups, and we’ve observed that groups are often nested, with subgroups inside of larger groups.

So now we’re ready to discuss the differing organizational paradigms that groups tend to use, depending on their size, compexity and purpose. And along with these differing structures, we will see corresponding realignment of individuals’ loyalties within these different structures.

  • Family is the foundational paradigm
  • Friends and colleagues expand the circle
  • Tribes organize even larger groups
  • Written culture allow groups to expand
  • Experts form peer groups
  • Multicultural societies embrace diverse subgroups
