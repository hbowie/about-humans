37. Cultures change based on group size, complexity and purpose.

We’ve already touched on the importance of cooperative groups in human life, and we’ve spoken of culture as providing the operating systems for groups, and we’ve observed that groups are often nested, with subgroups inside of larger groups.

So now we’re ready to discuss the differing organizational paradigms that groups tend to use, depending on their size, compexity and purpose. And along with these differing structures, we will see corresponding realignment of individuals’ feelings and attitudes within these different structures.

Group Name Purpose Structure Feelings Representative Thought
family Care and nurturing of one another Intimate knowledge of one another, regular face-to-face contact, inherited relationship patterns Strong feelings of loyalty to other family members Blood is thicker than water
camp, village, Dunbar group Working together for the good of the group Shared group norms, knowledge, stories and myths, communicated by example and verbally Respect for members, based on direct knowledge, proven strengths and weaknesses, and assigned roles
