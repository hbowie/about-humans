I. The Basics  ↑

12. Specific trust is built between individuals.

As we get to know individuals in our groups, we develop specific levels of trust based on three factors.

  1. Character — if we believe someone has character (or integrity, or authenticity), then we believe they will generally follow the expectations for fair play;

  2. Caring — we need to believe that someone actually includes us within their circle of care, that we are someone that they care about enough to offer help when needed;

  3. Competence — if a task requires some sort of specialization, then we need to believe in a person’s competence to perform that particular sort of task.
